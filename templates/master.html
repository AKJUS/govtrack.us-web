H.R. 5882’s committee report implies the American public can’t handle legislative information.

More than 1,000 letters have been written so far this week. Your office will be hearing from constituents shortly, if you haven’t already! {% if remote_net_house or remote_net_senate or request.user.username == "admin" %}Contact Joshua Tauberer, president of Civic Impulse LLC, at <tauberer@govtrack.us> or 202-558-7227 for more.{% endif %}

The committee report for H.R. 5882, the legislative branch appropriations bill for 2013, originally halted the publication of “bulk data downloads.” Now it creates a task force to study what we already know is feasible.

Speaker Boehner and Majority Leader Cantor have supported bulk legislative data throughout the 112th Congress. House Appropriations has invented techno-sounding concerns that the Republican leadership has already demonstrated are non-issues. (This is a non-partisan issue. Rep. Honda (D, CA) has supported bulk data too.)

Congressman Crenshaw doesn’t think Americans can handle this data.

More than 1,000 people wrote Congress this week about H.R. 5882. The bill’s committee report says Congress shouldn’t publish more raw data without funding to check that what the public does with the data is correct.

UPDATE: The House Appropriations committee has slightly revised their position. The committee previously wanted to stop publishing all “bulk legislative data.” Now they want to study the situation more. A new task force is not enough. More doing less talking about doing, please!

Step up and write your representative to fix the bill, or read more about what’s going on.

Take Action

Speaker Boehner and House Majority Leader Cantor have supported bulk legislative data over the last year. Rep. Honda has supported bulk data for many years. We need to show that the public cares about this issue too before the bill comes to a vote.
