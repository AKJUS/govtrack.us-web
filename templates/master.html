This is what GovTrack could look like
if H.R. 5882 passes.

The committee report for H.R. 5882, the legislative branch appropriations bill for 2013, instructs GPO to stop making available bulk data downloads of legislative data.

If you’ve ever found GovTrack useful, you’ve benefitted from bulk data downloads. We use bulk data from GPO and the House Clerk to make GovTrack what it is.

Speaker Boehner and Majority Leader Cantor have supported bulk legislative data throughout the 112th Congress, creating the new Docs.House.Gov and Real Time Floor Updates. House Appropriations has invented techno-sounding concerns that the Republican leadership has already demonstrated are non-issues. (This is a non-partisan issue. Rep. Honda (D, CA) has supported bulk data too.)

Learn more...

  • Testimony by Tom Bruce, director of Cornell’s Legal Information Institute, before the House Administration Committee, June 2011 and why this isn’t just about accountability
  • Comments by the Sunlight Foundation filed with House Appropriations earlier this year
  • Comments by GovTrack’s creator Josh Tauberer filed with Appropriations earlier this year
  • An explanation from the Sunlight Foundation about what is going on right now

Stay tuned...

H.R. 5882 could be brought up under suspension as early as Wednesday. Check back here for developments.
