Other Websites Reusing GovTrack Data

GovTrack isn’t just a website. It’s a reusable database of congressional information put out there for anyone to build new tools out of. We support open knowledge to the limit.

Who’s using our data and API?

Here’s a list of some of the websites and apps using our data or API:

  • OpenCongress: Quite like GovTrack with a focus on social networking-type tools. (Since 2007)
  • MAPLight.org: Computes correlations between votes and campaign contributions. (Since 2007)
  • Follow the Oil Money: A money-and-votes site directed at energy and oil.
  • WhereABill.org: Follow the progress of a bill through the capitol complex, visually, on a map. (Since 2007)
  • TheMiddleClass.org: Tracking legislation of significance to the middle class.
  • Filibusted.us: Who is filibustering the most bills? Sunlight Apps for America winner (2009).
  • International Foundation of Employee Benefit Plans
  • Congrelate
  • LittleSis.org
  • VoteReports: Use VoteReports to see if your representatives truly represent you (since 2010)
  • Congress111, by Mike Bluestein, has a variety of information on Members of Congress, based in part on GovTrack's database. (since 2010)
  • Almost half of the entries to Sunlight Foundation's Apps for America contest in 2009 used data from GovTrack
  • IBM ManyBills (since 2010)
  • Connect2Congress (since 2010)
  • GeoCongress.us (since 2010)
  • VoteSane (since 2010)
  • Watch the Vote
  • Votility (since 2010)
  • POPVOX (since 2010; uses our source code)
  • Votetocracy
  • DemCom, the House Democrats’s intranet (since 2011)
  • KillFil! - Kill Filibuster (since 2011)
  • VoterVoice.net (since 2011)
  • Agreement Groups in the United States Senate, by Adrien Friggeri (2012)
  • Real Clear Politics Congressional Bill Tracker (since 2013)
  • Informed American (since 2013)
  • Pocket Congress (since 2013)
  • Girl emPower iOS app (2013)
  • Words & Votes: Gun violence in the United States (since 2013)
  • Mapping Historical Votes: When Women's Suffrage Failed, 1915 by Xenocrypt (2013)
  • Orange County’s Election Legislation Tracker (since 2013)
  • eligo Congress iOS app (since 2013)
  • Voting Relationships between Senators (2013)
  • Informed American Windows Phone app (2014)
  • 113th U. S. Congress Android app (2014)
  • Polco: The Political Compass (2014)

Previously Used Our Data

These apps began by using our data, and later transitioned to using the data developed in the github:unitedstates/congress community project.

  • Sunlight Foundation's Real Time Congress API (initially the Drumbone API)
  • Congress app for Android phones by the Sunlight Foundation. (2010-2012)
  • Scout by the Sunlight Foundation (2012)
  • Call on Congress voice service app by the Sunlight Foundation (2012)

Archived List

Sites no longer operating:

  • National Journal: Committee Chairmen: An interactive game where you can play musical chairs with the committee leadership of the incoming 111th Congress.
  • Govit: Citizens vote on bills. (2010)
  • Laws I Like: Vote on bills and post your votes to your profile. (2009)
  • RepresentedBy: Post your representatives to your profile and keep up with what they're doing. (2009)
  • WeTheCandidates (2010)
  • NewBallot: You vote on the same laws Congress is voting on. Every week your representative gets a report on how their constituency wants them to vote. (2010)
  • WatchDog.net: Another GovTrack clone with some additional information. (2009)
  • CongressDB: Analysis of voting records.
  • PeepGov.com (2010)
  • Citizen Binder (2010)
