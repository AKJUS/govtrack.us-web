About GovTrack.us

Creating government transparency and civic engagement through novel uses of technology.

GovTrack.us, a project of Civic Impulse, LLC now in its 10th year, is one of the world ºs most visited government transparency websites. The site helps ordinary citizens find and track bills in the U.S. Congress and understand their representatives’ legislative record.

In 2013, GovTrack.us was used by 8 million individuals. We sent out 3 million legislative update email alerts. Our embeddable widgets were deployed on more than 80 official websites of Members of Congress.

We bring together the status of U.S. federal legislation, voting records, congressional district maps, and more (see the table at the right). and make it easier to understand. Use GovTrack to track bills for updates or get alerts about votes with email updates and RSS feeds. We also have unique statistical analyses to put the information in context. Read the «Analysis Methodology».

GovTrack openly shares the data it brings together so that other websites can build other tools to help citizens engage with government. See the «Developer Documentation» for more.

Who we are

GovTrack was created in 2004 by Joshua Tauberer originally as a hobby. Today it is a project of Civic Impulse, LLC, his company. See our «Press Clippings» to read what people think of the site.

GovTrack isn’t affiliated with the government, and our only funding comes from advertising. You can read our «Advertising Policy».

To send us a note, email operations@govtrack.us.

Our staff and contributors have included Aviad Eilam, Gordon P. Hemsley, Village Creative, Ben Rellick, Daniel Gabriele, Grigoriy Petukhov, Patrick Tutwiler, and Kevin Henry.

The Open Government / Open Government Data / Government Transparency Movements

The open government movement involves citizens, web developers, designers, researchers, writers, statisticians, government managers, and elected officials in a concerted effort to make data a national asset, to make government more transparent and effective, and to empower citizens to participate effectively in governance and in their communities.

Since our launch in 2004, GovTrack has been a leader in the growing and changing open government movement. GovTrack’s creator, Josh, also works on the policy angle of open government data. He wrote about it in the book Open Government Data: The Book (2012).

Join our movement. Here are some resources:

  • Sunlight Labs Google Group: A community email group for anyone working on civic technology.
  • Transparency Camp Conference: The annual "un-"conference in DC on government transparency and civic engagement from a technology perspective.
  • The Declaration on Parliamentary Openness: An international declaration on the roles of parliaments and parliamentary-monitoring organizations (like GovTrack does for Congress).
  • Open Government Data: The Book: A book by GovTrack’s creator on the open government data movement.

Current Information
(113th Congress)
Bill Status From THOMAS.gov. Updated daily. Legislative activity appears the next business day.
Votes From the Senate and House websites. Updated hourly.
Bill Text From THOMAS.gov, GPO.gov/FDSys, and Cato Institute Deepbills. Updated daily, but text only becomes available typically 1-3 business days after a bill is filed or engrossed.
Upcoming Bills From the Senate website and Docs.House.Gov. Updated daily.
Upcoming Committee Meetings From the Senate website and Docs.House.Gov. Updated daily.
State Legislation From LegiNation, Inc., LegiScan, Inc, and OpenStates. Updated weekly.
Most of the information above is sourced through github:unitedstates/congress, a community project we help develop.
