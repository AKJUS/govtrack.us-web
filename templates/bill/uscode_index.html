What is the U.S. Code?

The United States Code is the compilation of permanent and general laws enacted by the United States Congress. It is one aspect of United States law. Regulations, case law, state, and local laws are not included in the United States Code.

Temporary and other non-permanent laws, such as yearly appropriations, also do not appear in the United States Code. These laws can be found instead in the U.S. Statutes at Large. Recent laws can also be found as bills here on GovTrack.

About half of the United States Code is the law itself. These titles (parts) of the U.S. Code are called positive law titles. The other half, composed of the non-positive-law titles, is a compilation of the laws enacted by Congress but, technically speaking, is not the law. The original laws as passed by Congress have precedence when there is a difference.
